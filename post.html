The Importance of Emotional Intelligence

"The Seven Principles for Making Marriage Work"

Written by John M. Gottham on March 16, 1999

While learning about eternal relationships, one pivotal lesson stood out to me: the importance of emotional intelligence. While reading "The Seven Principles for Making Marriage Work," I learned a profound truth that resonated deeply with me.

As humans, we are inherently emotional beings, and our emotions play a fundamental role in shaping the fabric of our connections. Yet, it is imperative that we learn to wield these emotions through striving to become emotionally intelligent. At its core, emotional intelligence entails the skill of tempering negative thoughts and feelings about others, preventing them from overshadowing the positive aspects of our relationships.

Emotional intelligence is not only crucial for marital harmony but also for nurturing thriving partnerships in every sphere of life—whether familial, platonic, or professional. As highlighted in the book: "The more emotionally intelligent a couple is—the better able they are to understand, honor, and respect each other and their marriage—the more likely they are to indeed live happily ever after."

The cultivation of emotional intelligence lays the groundwork for the construction of resilient and fulfilling relationships. It empowers us to navigate interactions with others with grace and insight, fostering deep understanding, effective communication, unwavering empathy, and skillful conflict resolution.

By nurturing emotional intelligence, we equip ourselves with a powerful tool for profound connection and enduring love, paving the way for mutual respect, harmony, and joy in our relationships.
